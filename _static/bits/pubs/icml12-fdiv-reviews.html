Detailed comments for the authors Recently, the variational representations of f-divergences have gained popularity in the problem of estimating f-divergences from two sets of finite samples drawn iid from two distributions. In this paper, the authors improved the existing lower bound on these variational representations, and then derived a RKHS based algorithm to estimate the f-divergence between two distributions. With the improved lower bound, the authors empirically demonstrated that the corresponding empirical estimator performs better than the existing ones.

The paper is well-written and the results are interesting, except for few typos (see line 272: 2 should be written as (2); line 276: replace (1.2) by (2); see the derivation of \phi*(r) on the right column of page 4, where the signs of the second terms in each of the lines have to be interchanged--please check). The discussing on the relation to MMD is very interesting and I appreciate the authors for highlighting this connection. While the improvement in empirical results are marginal over the existing results, the paper is overall interesting from a theoretical point of view.
Summary of evaluation / Paper's main strengths and weaknesses The authors improved the existing lower bound on the variational representations of f-divergences and then derived an RKHS based algorithm to estimate the f-divergence between two distributions from finite samples. They also established a nice connection between the problem of estimating f-divergences and maximum mean discrepancy using the kernel algorithm they derived. While the empirical results show only a marginal improvement over the existing results, the paper is theoretically interesting.

Detailed comments for the authors The authors proposed a variational method to estimate f-divergences. The proposed method improves the work of (Nguyen,07). The authors derived a tighter lower-bound on the variation representation of f-divergence.

The paper is readable, but certain parts could be improved. Some comments are listed below.

The results are impressive, but still in my opinion the work is somewhat incremental compared to (Nguyen,07). The ultimate minimization problem (8) is very similar to the problem at the end of Lemma3 of (Nguyen, 07). The main difference is that (8) optimizes alpha on a simplex, while (Nguyen, 07) optimizes unconstrained alpha.

Numerical experiments confirm that the variational estimators are good when the true divergence is small, but I think this section could be improved by studying other distributions as well that are truly high-dimensional. It would also be great to see comments on why higher dimensionality causes problems.

Detailed comments for the authors This article provides tighter bounds on the variational representation for the family of f-divergences when applied to probability measures. The new bound is obtained by taking into account the fact that f-divergences are defined between probability distributions. The authors also describe an optimization program to compute f-divergences between empirical distributions, and relate their program to the Maximum Mean discrepancy of (Gretton et al, 2008).
The paper is very well written, with compelling theoretical and empirical results. It should be of interest for the ML community.
